Stroke Education for Patients

A stroke happens when blood flow to the brain is blocked or a blood vessel bursts. It can cause serious problems like weakness, trouble speaking, or memory loss. But the good news? Many strokes can be prevented or treated if caught early.

Key Facts:

  • What is a stroke? It's like a "brain attack." Blood carries oxygen—without it, brain cells die quickly.
  • Signs to watch for: Sudden numbness, confusion, trouble seeing, dizziness, or severe headache. Remember "FAST": Face drooping, Arm weakness, Speech slurred, Time to call 911.
  • Prevention: Control blood pressure, eat healthy, exercise, don't smoke, and manage diabetes.
  • Treatment: Time is critical—get to the hospital fast for clot-busting drugs or surgery.
