Featured article

When a few minutes can change a stroke outcome

One of the most important ideas in stroke care is that time matters. Sudden speech change, weakness, numbness, imbalance, or vision loss should never be observed at home for hours while waiting to see if it improves.

Fast recognition leads to faster imaging, earlier evaluation, and better access to time-sensitive therapies. For patients and families, the first educational message is simple: if symptoms are sudden, act urgently.

  • Stroke symptoms may not be painful, but they are still emergent.
  • Even temporary symptoms can be clinically important.
  • Calling emergency services early can improve treatment options.

Know the warning signs: F.A.S.T.

Keeping this section visible makes the website useful to both new visitors and returning readers.

F

Face

Check for new facial droop or numbness. Ask the person to smile.

A

Arm

Ask the person to raise both arms. One may drift or feel weak.

S

Speech

Speech may sound slurred, confused, or suddenly difficult to produce.

T

Time

Call 911 immediately. Sudden symptoms require urgent medical attention.
