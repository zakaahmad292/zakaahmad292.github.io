First article

When a few minutes can change a stroke outcome

One of the most important lessons in stroke care is that time matters. Sudden speech change, weakness, numbness, imbalance, or vision loss should never be watched at home for hours while waiting to see if it improves.

Fast recognition leads to faster imaging, earlier evaluation, and better access to time-sensitive therapies. For patients and families, the core message is simple: if symptoms are sudden, act urgently.

  • Stroke symptoms may not be painful, but they are still emergencies.
  • Even symptoms that improve can still be clinically important.
  • Early emergency evaluation may improve treatment options.
